Wires, Cables and WiFi

Figuring out the ways we connect to other and how others connect to us.

Intro Wires Cables WiFi

The internet is similar to a postal office but instead of boxes or packages the internet ships binary information. his information is made of bits.

    • Bits- Can be described as any pair of opposites like on or off or in this case 1 and 0. 8 bits is 1 byte, 1000 byte is 1 Kilobyte, 1000 Kilobyte is 1 Megabyte. for reference a song is typically encoded in 3-4 Megabytes. Everything is sent in bits because they are considered the atoms of information.
    • Bandwidth- Transmission capacity, measured by the bit rate
    • Bitrate- The number of bits per second a system can transmit
    • Latency- Time it takes a bit to travel from sender to receiver

In today's world, we typically send bits through 3 methods (Electricity, Light, and Radio)

    • Electricity- This form of information transport is found in ethernet cords which is cheaper however to signal starts to get lost after a 100ft
    • Light- Is much faster than electricity however it is more expensive and very difficult to work with. Fiber Optic cables are used to connect different countries' internet.
    • Radio- Can be found in WIFI and have very short range because after a certain distance they are lost, think of how you can turn a radio to a station in LA from New York. The signal is translated to a frequency and then translated back to binary when they reach the destination receiver.

The most popular method of transmitting binary information is through a wired connection. Wires such as the ethernet cable run the binary information from your computer to the desired location. The pros of using wires is that they are cheap and rituality available however after a few hundred feet the signal is lost.

Cables are used to transport light which is much faster than electricity with wires. These cables are known as fiber optic cables and they are threads of glass engineered to send light beams. When a beam of light is sent through the fiber optic cable the light bounds up and down the length of the cable until it is received on the other end. Additionally, depending on the bound angle, you could send multiple bytes simultaneously all traveling at the speed of light.

Unlike wires these cables can run a signal for miles without disruption because they can travel so far, and so fast fiber optic cables are actually used to connect one continent to other by running a cable along the sea floor. However, the downside is that these cables are crazy expensive to make, hard to work with and very fragile to use.

So how do we move information with out wires? Through the power of radio frequencies. Wireless bit sending machines typically use a radio signal to send bit information from one place to another. The machines actually translate the 1 and 0s into a frequency that is then sent to another machine known as the receiver.
