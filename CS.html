Cyber Security

In today's society cyber crimes cause major problems, personal, financially and even in matters of national security. Cyber Security helps us fight against these crimes.

Introduction Cyber Crimes Prevention
Within the last few years hundreds of millions of credit cards numbers have been stolen, along with tens of millions of social security numbers and health care records. Even nuclear center fusions have been hacked. This are all compromised by the hackers exploiting vulnerabilities in hardware and software and in more cases unintentional decisions by people using the software.
People who commit cyber crimes do not fit a single profile motivation; it could vary from an international terrorist to a teenager looking to prove himself. In today’s world, many developed nations do not only have a regular army but also a cyber army. Experts speculate that the next World War might be fought with computer attacks targeting resources, communications, transportation systems, and electric grids. Different types of Attacks:

Viruses
A virus is a program that gets installed usually unintentionally and harms the user and the computer. Additionally, it is possible for a virus to spread itself to other computers. For a person to get a computer virus there are a couple of methods. One method is through a fake installation usually saying you need a protection program against viruses. Another method is through a fault in a security back door where it can install itself without permission. When a virus gets on your computer it can steal and or delete all your information, control programs, and even remote operations.

DDoS
Hackers using computer viruses can take over millions of computers worldwide and then use those computers as an army is known as a botnet to attack and take down websites. This type of attack is known as a Distributed Denial of Service or DDoS. A denial of service is when hackers overwhelm a website server with too many requests and is referred to as distributed when the attack comes from many computers all at once. Most servers are ready for millions of requests a day however when billions of trillions of requests coming from different locations, the server then becomes overloaded.

Phishing
Another trick from cybercriminals is to send millions of spam emails to deceive people into sharing sensitive personal information or otherwise known as a phishing scam. A Phishing scam is typically when you receive an email that seems like a trustworthy email asking you to sign into your account but clicking the e email will take you to a fake website where you login in your information and thus giving the hacker your personal information. From there hackers can use your credentials to access your real accounts to steal information and even steal money.
Although we have organizations to fight against these crimes they are often not enough. People think when a system gets hacker that it was the fault of the security design or the software but 90% of the time it was caused by a simple human error. Luckily there are steps you can take to protect yourself and those around you:
    • Use strong passwords.
    • Check for authentic web addresses.
    • Install system security updates often.
    • Don't install software you don't trust.
